# Name Options for the Project

**Core story**: Private, hidden, sacred yield on Zama. Encrypted positions that only the owner ("the initiated") can see or enter. Ancient temple / treasury metaphor — innermost chambers, hidden vaults, secret places where wealth and offerings were kept away from the public eye.

# Final Decision

**Celano** (chosen July 6, 2026)

- Real historic town in Abruzzo, Italy (L'Aquila province).
- Famous for its medieval castle (Castello Piccolomini di Celano).
- User wanted something Italian, more substantial than short names like Naos, and preferably non-Greek.
- We are using it for the "protected historic place / castle that keeps what matters inside" metaphor.

Focus below: Latin (Roman), Etruscan-influenced Latin terms, and a few strong Italian-historic roots. Emphasis on longer, weightier names that feel premium and historic.

**Legal / Naming Note on "Celano"**

Celano is a real small Italian comune (town). There are a handful of unrelated Italian companies using the name (construction, ceramics, energy). There was a past trademark dispute in Vietnam over an ice cream brand — unrelated to finance or software.

For a Zama hackathon submission (demo on Sepolia, presented as a community project), the practical risk is very low. No realistic chance of "sanctions" or legal action before the July 7 deadline.

If this ever becomes a real company/product later, you may want to:
- Check EU/Italian trademark databases
- Consider adding a distinguishing word (e.g. Celano Yield, Celano Protocol)
- Or treat it as a codename and rebrand for mainnet launch

For now: we're shipping with Celano. It sounds good, feels Italian, and has the castle story we can lean on.
